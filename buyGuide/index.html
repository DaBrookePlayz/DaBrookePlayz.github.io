Buying Guide

A Thing To Note

For the vast majority of computer users, you DO NOT need to build your own PC. If you do not feel comfortable dealing with computer parts, then just buy a pre-built computer, or even a laptop. While pre-built towers tend to be more expensive than just building it on your own, one can easily break components or get electrocuted by improperly building a PC. Custom-built PCs are also typically meant for those who need a very high performance computer with expensive parts.

For Portability

Dell Latitude 7440 Laptop (1400$: vPro management disabled, 256GB SSD, no fingerprint reader, 3-cell 57-Wh boost-capable battery)
- Suitable for college students and those who travel frequently
- Cannot smoothly run games or applications that require many resources
- Tend to be more difficult to repair
- Works out of the box (for the most part)

For The Artists

Mac Studio (2000$: 38 core GPU, 32GB RAM, 512GB SSD, no pre-installed software)
- Suitable for Adobe, Apple and others products requiring graphical or audio processing
- Power efficient for its processing power
- On the pricer end for computers
- Proprietary OS has a lack of backwards-compatability, especially with games

"It Just Works"

Amso Desktop Tower PC (125$)
- Meant for those who aren't experienced in the tech field - Very easy to troubleshoot due to durability and non-complexity - Extremely inexpensive due its low specifications - Only browsers and Microsoft utilities run well on it
